# Payer pitch deck — canonical narrative arc

Extracted from `payer pitch decks/`, primarily the **BCBS FL deck (March 2024)** and the **Marley Medical Payer Deck Feb 2024** — both of which represent the matured 2024 payer narrative. Earlier decks (UHC Accelerator May 2023, OSF March 2024) follow the same arc with payer-specific adjustments.

The Marley payer pitch is a **3-act structure** within a topics-for-discussion framing:

1. **Marley Medical Overview** — what we are, the problem we solve, our model, our evidence
2. **How Marley Works with Payers** — fit to payer challenges, contracting structures, easy-to-work-with framing
3. **Appendix: Clinical Data Deep Dive** — outcome data in depth, geographic expansion

---

## Act 1 — Marley Medical Overview

### Slide 1 — Cover
- **Marley Medical** wordmark logo
- **Prepared for [Payer Name]**
- **Date**

### Slide 2 — Topics for Discussion
Brief agenda (3 bullets matching the 3 acts).

### Slide 3 — Welcome / What Marley is
Three-bullet definition:
- We are a virtual-first medical practice designed specifically for patients with cardiometabolic disease
- We manage **hypertension, type 2 diabetes, lipid disorders and obesity/overweight** with a team-based and integrated care model
- We currently bill fee-for-service, but are increasingly contracting for outcomes-based contracts

### Slide 4 — The problem (hero stats slide)
Four canonical stats arranged in a 2×2 with sources cited:
- **60+%** of patients with cardiometabolic disease are uncontrolled
- **2/3** of hospitalizations are avoidable
- **<7%** of patients have optimal cardiometabolic health
- **26+** days to see a provider

Plus spend framing:
- **$600B** annual spend on CV and Endocrine
- **23%** of total patients = **71%** of total healthcare spend
- **2–3x** annual spend on cardiometabolic chronic disease vs. those without

### Slide 5 — Primary care is overburdened
"PCPs need **26.7 hours per day** to provide all guideline-recommended primary care services to a panel of 2,500 US adults."

### Slide 6 — A new category is needed
Three-bullet framing of why current options fail and where Marley fits:
- Complex cardiometabolic patients need more access to primary and specialty care → today served by specialists
- Care needs to be integrated and coordinated across providers, testing, procedures → new category: **Comprehensive & Integrated Care for Chronic Disease (paid for outcomes)**
- Outcomes-based payment models needed → today served by PCPs

### Slide 7 — What Marley is (the integrated cardiometabolic clinic)
Three-bullet definition:
- Multidisciplinary care team provides comprehensive, team-based care
- We deliver evidence-based care and can diagnose, treat, prescribe and coordinate with local providers
- We are value-based and tie our fees to enrollment and clinical outcomes

### Slide 8 — Comprehensive clinical scope + current panel profile
Two columns:
- **Conditions managed:** Hypertension, Type 2 diabetes, Pre-diabetes, High cholesterol, Obesity
- **Current panel profile:** Age (mean/max), Chronic conditions (mean/median), Mental health %, Obesity %

### Slide 9 — The 20–30% we serve
Visual positioning: Marley sits between specialists, PCPs, and care managers — for the segment of cardiometabolic patients not well served by current options. We get paid for outcomes.

### Slide 10 — Three ways patients come to Marley
1. Patient self-refers (direct acquisition)
2. PCP refers to Marley
3. Payer identifies patients for Marley

Plus the navigation logic: Marley refers to higher-level specialty care when needed (imaging, procedures).

### Slide 11 — Patient journey
Simple horizontal flow: Lead → Video visit → Ship kit → Continuous monitoring → Proactive outreach + asynchronous messaging + lifestyle intervention → Follow-up video visits.
> "We average 2+ patient touch points a week, keeping patients motivated and engaged."

### Slide 12 — Engagement metrics
Four hero metrics:
- **78%** of patients active in last 30 days
- **<1 week** access to first MD visit
- **75%** retention at 1 year
- **5** device readings per week on average
- **2.5** messages read per patient in last 7 days
- **86** NPS score in last 30 days

### Slide 13 — Data-driven care model + Propeller heritage
"Our team spent 10 years building and validating data-driven and high-engagement care models at Propeller Health."

Comparable evidence:
- **57%** reduction in ED + Hospitalizations (Asthma, at Propeller)
- **33%** reduction in ED + Hospitalizations (COPD, at Propeller)

### Slide 14 — Recent patient case example
Concrete patient case showing rapid intervention: from Hypertensive Crisis (e.g. 182/117) to Controlled BP (e.g. 126/72) in ~23 days with 2 medication changes + lifestyle changes + engagement. Annotated timeline showing each intervention.

### Slide 15 — Clinical results
- **70%** at guideline-based clinical goal at 6 months
- **14.8 mmHg** average BP reduction at 12 weeks (Stage 2 HTN entry)
- Downstream risk reduction (citing Lancet meta-analysis): For every 10mmHg reduction in BP — 20% less risk of major CV events, 27% less risk of stroke, 28% less risk of CHF.

## Act 2 — How Marley Works with Payers

### Slide 16 — Marley meets payer needs (2-column slide)
Left column: what Marley is designed to address —
- A focus on an expensive patient population
- Comprehensive care model with high engagement
- Ability to control a significant portion of medical spend
- Ability to selectively refer to in-person care, when needed

Right column: the payer challenges we solve —
- **Engagement** — Up to 30% of an MA membership is not engaged. We can bring them back.
- **Star Ratings** — Based on chronic disease control and NPS. We improve both.
- **MLR** — Polychronic patients are 70% of the cost. We reduce specialty and in-patient spend.

### Slide 17 — Propeller heritage as proof of payer competence
"Our team knows how to partner with payers to generate great outcomes." Co-founders + ~⅓ of team from Propeller Health. Logos of payers Marley has worked with at Propeller (and now at Marley).

### Slide 18 — We solve the biggest payer challenges (summary, 4 boxes)
- **Access** — for complex patients falling through the cracks
- **Engagement** — hard-to-engage and expensive patients with cardiometabolic disease
- **Outcomes** — high patient satisfaction, improved clinical outcomes, HEDIS and Star metrics
- **Positive ROI** — reduce unnecessary utilization, reduce total cost of care, generate positive ROI

### Slide 19 — Easy to work with (3 boxes)
- **Broad population** — not just 1–2 diseases or the highly motivated
- **Guideline-based** — evidence and guideline-based medicine, programmatically
- **Highly scalable** — tech-enabled care model scales by adding new licensed providers

Plus three additional credibility boxes:
- **Easy contracting** — already have active provider agreements with BCBS in several states
- **Value-based** — we want to get paid for the outcomes we generate; we will put our fees at risk
- **Path to geographic expansion** — easy except for payer contracting and credentialing, which payer partners help with

### Slide 20 — The three stages of program design
- Patient selection — we will help you identify patients who are good candidates
- Outcomes-based billing structures — we have 3 common contracting structures
- Evaluation design — we will work with you to select the key clinical and HEDIS measures

### Slide 21 — Patient selection criteria
Four criteria:
1. **Patient meets clinical profile** — ICD-10 group for inclusion / exclusion
2. **Patient is not attributed in VBC** — not attributed to PCP in HMO/VBC/ACO/risk arrangement
3. **Patient is not engaged in care** — no PCP claim in 12–18 months, no cardiometabolic Rx in last 6 months
4. **Patient is failing management** — 2+ ED visits in last 12 months, 1+ inpatient episode in last 12 months

### Slide 22 — Three contracting structures
1. **FFS + Quality Bonus (+/-)** — standard FFS claims, +/-30% bonus at 12 months based on quality
2. **Capitated Rate + Fees at Risk** — monthly capitated rate, billed on active patients, up to 30% at risk
3. **Submit Claims Based on Milestones** — agreed clinical milestones (enrollment, active in month, reaches goal, Stars/HEDIS metrics)

### Slides 23–25 — Each contracting structure visualized
Standalone slide for each structure showing the monthly claim flow and the evaluation point.

## Act 3 — Appendix: Clinical Data Deep Dive

This appendix expands the clinical evidence with more granular cuts:
- BP classification distribution at intake (Stage 1 / Stage 2 / Hypertensive Crisis)
- Average BP reductions by entry stage
- % of patients with >10 mmHg drop, by time on platform
- BP reduction by time on platform — all patients, Stage 2 entry, Stage 3 entry
- Weight change by time on platform (overall and on weight-meds-only)
- Geographic expansion plan (state business registration → clinician licensing → payer enrollment)
- Comparable evidence — Ochsner Digital Medicine Program cost savings ($204 PMPM HTN, $163 PMPM T2DM)
- Cost of uncontrolled hypertension benchmarks ($39k PCI, $77.7k CABG, $37k stroke 1st-quarter costs)

## Payer-specific customization

Each deck is **prepared for a specific named payer**. Customize:
- Cover slide (payer name + date)
- Any state-specific evidence to the payer's primary states
- "Easy contracting" slide: reference BCBS contracts if pitching another BCBS plan; reference Anthem if pitching Anthem affiliate, etc.
- B2B pipeline / current payer-relationships slide reflects the payer's competitive context
