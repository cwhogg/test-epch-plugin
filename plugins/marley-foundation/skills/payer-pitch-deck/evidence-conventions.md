# Evidence conventions — payer decks

How Marley cites clinical evidence, economic claims, and outcomes data in payer-facing materials. Drawn from how the 2024 payer decks actually do this.

---

## Clinical-outcome claims

### What we cite about ourselves
- **70% at guideline-based clinical goal at 6 months**
- **14.8 mmHg average BP reduction at 12 weeks** (Stage 2 HTN entry)
- Distribution: % patients with >10 mmHg drop, broken out by time on platform AND by entry BP stage
- **Always include the date stamp** ("As of Nov 27, 2023") — outcomes are point-in-time

### What we cite about the broader literature
- **Downstream risk reduction from 10 mmHg BP reduction**, citing:
  > *Ettehad et al. "Blood pressure lowering for prevention of cardiovascular disease and death: a systematic review and meta-analysis". Lancet. Dec 2015; 387 (1022)*
  - 20% less risk of major CV events
  - 27% less risk of stroke
  - 28% less risk of CHF
  - 17% less risk of CHD
  - 13% less risk of all-cause mortality
  - Sample size: n=614,000 patients

### How we frame the comparison
- "Our results show that our model is working in our population"
- "We get over 50% of our patients to guideline-indicated clinical goals within 2 months, with over 70% at goal at 6 months."
- Numerical claims paired with the cohort definition ("Stage 2 HTN entry" not just "our patients")

## Economic / ROI claims

### Our own economic claims (what we can claim)
- We currently bill fee-for-service. Specific PPPM rates and gross margins may be shared with sophisticated payer audiences but should be reserved until later in the conversation.
- We are willing to put our fees at risk (up to 30%) on quality metrics.

### Comparable evidence we cite
- **Ochsner Digital Medicine Program** cost savings:
  - HTN: $204 PMPM
  - T2DM: $163 PMPM
- **Cost of acute cardiovascular events** (Clinicoecon Outcomes Res. 2015 Jun 9;7:337–45):
  - $39,000 for PCI
  - $77,701 for CABG
  - $37,000 for stroke (first-quarter)

### Market-level economic framing
- **23% of total patients account for 71% of total healthcare spend** (Cardiometabolic) — frames the patient population's economic significance.
- **$600B annual spend on CV and Endocrine.**
- **2–3x annual spend** on cardiometabolic chronic disease patients vs. those without.

## How we handle outcomes claims we don't yet have

We don't make claims we can't back. The payer decks consistently:
- Use **comparable evidence from adjacent chronic-disease programs** (Propeller's asthma/COPD results, Ochsner Digital Medicine) when Marley doesn't yet have N-of-Marley evidence
- Frame Propeller results explicitly as comparable: "Our team spent 10 years building and validating data-driven and high-engagement care models at Propeller Health."
- **Don't extrapolate** Propeller results to Marley patient population without naming the leap

## How we cite sources

Standard format in payer decks:
- Source line at bottom of slide in small type
- Format: *Author et al. "Title". Journal. Date; volume(issue)*
- For agency/government sources: *Organization. Title. Date.*

Citation sources used in 2024 payer decks:
- Gerteis et al. (AHRQ Multiple Chronic Conditions Chartbook)
- KFF Health Costs / Disease-spend perspective
- O'Hearn et al. (J Am Coll Cardiol 2022)
- AMN/Merritt Hawkins 2022 Survey of Physician Appointment Wait Times
- AHRQ Potentially Avoidable Hospitalizations
- Porter et al. (J Gen Intern Med 38(1) — the 26.7 hrs/day stat)
- Ettehad et al. (Lancet 2015 BP meta-analysis)
- Clinicoecon Outcomes Res 2015 (CV event cost data)

## What NOT to claim in payer materials

- Don't claim cost savings figures from Marley's own program until we have published methodology and adequate N
- Don't claim NPS without sample size and date window
- Don't claim "improvement" without baseline and time window
- Don't use vague qualifiers ("significant", "substantial", "industry-leading") — payer audiences read these as noise

---

## How we discuss limitations

When evidence is thin or comparable rather than direct:
- Name what's Marley-direct vs. comparable-evidence: "Our team's prior results at Propeller (asthma/COPD)…"
- Acknowledge the size/maturity of the current Marley dataset ("As of [date], n=X")
- Lead with what we DO have, then bound it appropriately
