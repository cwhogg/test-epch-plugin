# Marley Logo Usage

The Marley brand has three logo lockup types, each available in six colors. Files live in `assets/logos/` (curated SVG set in the plugin) and in the full library at `visual brand/Brand Assets/Marley Logos/`.

---

## Lockup types

### 1. Wordmark
The full "Marley Medical" wordmark. The primary brand lockup. Use this:
- On title slides
- On the home page / landing pages
- In email headers
- In press materials
- Anywhere the company name needs to be present visually

Files: `assets/logos/MarleyMed_Logo_RGB_Wordmark_<Color>.svg`

### 2. Monogram
The "M" monogram. Use this:
- App icons
- Favicons
- Social-media profile images
- Sign-offs / signatures
- When the wordmark is too wide for the available space
- Internal documents where the brand presence should be quieter

Files: `assets/logos/MarleyMed_Logo_RGB_Monogram_<Color>.svg`

### 3. Icon-locked logo (full library only)
The wordmark paired with one of the brand icons (bandage, capsule, heart, stethoscope, tablet). A more expressive logo treatment. Use these:
- Themed sub-brand surfaces (e.g. clinical content might lock with the stethoscope)
- Marketing campaigns with a specific theme
- Conference / event materials

Files: in the full library at `visual brand/Brand Assets/Marley Logos/Digital/SVG/Icons/<Color>/` — not copied into plugin assets to keep the bundle small. Reference the source path when needed.

---

## Color treatments

Each lockup is available in **six colors**, matched to the brand palette in [[colors]]:

| Color | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Purple** (primary) | Default. On cream, white, peach, sky, or any light surface. |
| **Red** | Energetic / accent contexts. Animated brand moments. Use sparingly. |
| **Peach** | Reverse on dark-purple surfaces. Warm-themed campaigns. |
| **Sky** | Reverse on dark-purple surfaces. Cool/clinical contexts. |
| **Black** | Monochrome contexts: B&W print, single-color faxes, legal documents. |
| **White** | Reverse on dark / photographic surfaces. Always white when overlaid on purple. |

Animated versions (Purple GIF, Red GIF) exist in `visual brand/Brand Assets/Marley Logos/Animated/` for digital surfaces where animation is supported.

## Clear space and minimum size

The source brand library does not provide explicit clear-space or minimum-size rules. Apply this convention until formalized guidance lands:

- **Clear space:** at minimum, an empty margin equal to the height of the "M" in the wordmark around all sides of the lockup.
- **Minimum size:**
  - Wordmark: no smaller than 20px tall on digital, 0.5" tall on print.
  - Monogram: no smaller than 16px tall on digital, 0.25" tall on print.

If a surface needs the logo smaller than the wordmark's minimum, switch to the monogram.

---

## What NOT to do

- **Don't re-color the logo** outside the six brand colors. If the surface doesn't work with any of the six, change the surface, not the logo.
- **Don't add effects** — no drop shadows, no glows, no outlines, no gradients.
- **Don't rotate the logo.** Always horizontal.
- **Don't squish or stretch the lockup.** Use SVG at native aspect ratio.
- **Don't reset "Marley Medical" in Reckless** as a substitute for the wordmark. The wordmark uses its own letterforms; typing the name in any font does not substitute.
- **Don't place the logo on a busy photograph** without an underlying solid surface for clear space.
- **Don't use purple logo on dark backgrounds** or any non-cream/white/peach/sky surface — switch to white reverse instead.
- **Don't combine wordmark + monogram** in the same surface (pick one).
