# Decision Memo — Marley canonical template

**Source:** `memos/TEMPLATE - Decision Memo.docx` — Marley's own internal template for Decision Memos.

This is the canonical structure. Every Marley general-variant memo (the Decision Memo format) follows this. Copy this template to start a new memo.

---

The intention of the Decision Memo is to clearly articulate what decision was made and why. It should provide sufficient context to be read by anyone within Marley while still being concise.

Though the draft forms for comment could be in multiple forms (images, docs, decks), the final decision should live as an artifact within Notion and reference out relevant sources of information.

This template outlines the structure of a Decision Memo.

---

# Decision Memo: [question or topic]

## Goal
[Explain why we are making a decision on the question or topic. How is it important to Marley?]

## Stakeholders
[Explain who or what is impacted by this decision. Use RACI format to name decision rights explicitly:]

- **Responsible:** [name(s) doing the execution work]
- **Accountable:** [single decider — the one whose call this is]
- **Consulted:** [people whose input is sought before deciding]
- **Informed:** [people who need to know after the decision is made]

## Context
[Put the decision in its broader context: what have we previously decided that impacts this or will we have to decide in the future based on this? How does it connect to the business? What are some core considerations that matter?]

## Decision
[Write the decision as a declarative statement. Include a summary of "why".]

## Rationale
[Describe the framework that was applied to come to this decision. It should be a logical detailing, with references to supporting evidence where helpful. Similarly, it should also include the risks of making this choice and potential mitigants of those risks. Someone should be able to read the Rationale and see why the Decision was made, given the Context.]

## Alternatives Considered
[High-level list of alternatives not taken — optional. Where relevant, include the evidence for/against these choices as well.]

## Final Decision Date & Decider
[Details on who signed off on the decision and when.]
