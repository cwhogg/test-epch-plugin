## tl;dr

We are happy to report that we are still on plan, feel like we are making good progress and have had nothing go horribly wrong, yet. We have set a target launch date, where we will begin enrolling patients into our Marley Medical PC, of end of Q1 2022. We have completed a state-based go-to-market analysis, and have identified a group of states that look advantageous for Marley. From that group we have selected Michigan and South Carolina as our first two launch states. We have assessed a number of specific chronic conditions, validated through marketing acquisition tests, and have selected hypertension as our first disease to target. We are now working on the many items needed to prepare for launch — PC setup, clinical protocol development, enhanced patient acquisition tests, product MVP definition and build, EMR selection, company and product branding, and hiring our initial little team who will make it all happen. Sounds like a lot when you write it all down, but we feel confident in our ability to get it all done.

We are ending 2021 with a good start to Marley Medical, and a good plan going into 2022. Next year, 2022, will be a pivotal year in setting the trajectory for Marley Medical's future, and we are all excited to get started.

## V0.1 — Marketing tests and selection of first chronic condition

We have completed marketing acquisition tests in 4 different diseases, with similar (but tailored) ad campaigns, landing pages and survey flows for each specific disease. Landing pages for: Hypertension, Hypothyroidism, Heart Failure, Atrial Fibrillation.

We have selected **Hypertension** as our first GTM chronic condition. In addition to favorable marketing tests and good cost per lead, hypertension has many advantages for a virtual-first care approach:

- **Significant, interested patient population.** Hypertension represents a very large addressable population, with a prevalence of 45% in the 18+ age group and 75% of 60+, with only 25% attaining guideline-based treatment goal. Additionally, the solid CAC and conversion rates from our marketing tests demonstrate reachability and interest of this population.

- **Good opportunity for immediate commercialization.** Patients with hypertension have annual outpatient costs of ~$2,800, providing a significant pool of existing revenue to capture. Moreover, if we fully bill RPM (~$1,400/yr) as our primary revenue, we can still demonstrate some initial value compared to standard care. Long-term, annual inpatient spend of ~$3,000, and total cost of $9,000 per year, show opportunity to drive savings in value-based relationships.

- **Clinical care is prime for virtual.** Hypertension is almost exclusively medication-managed through known treatment algorithms determined by blood pressure, a primary endpoint easily captured at home, making it exceptionally portable to virtual care.

## V1.0 Project Status — Learning Goals (excerpt)

A key near-term goal is to continue to validate a few of our core hypotheses, namely that we can pull people through a funnel from ad to scheduled F2F visit and that the Marley offering is valuable to patients.

Learning goals for the SteadyMD partnership test:

1. **Conversion rate from ad to F2F visit:** where and why are patients dropping off in the funnel, when no cost and when paid
2. **Who is clicking:** demographics, clinical archetypes, clinical complexity
3. **Why they are clicking:** value propositions that resonated
4. **Insights into patient product needs for V2 launch (the Marley PC)**

## What to learn from this example

- **The tl;dr is a single dense paragraph** — pre-launch, the period doesn't yet have a clean headline, so the tl;dr is a state-of-the-company narrative.
- **"We have had nothing go horribly wrong, yet."** — characteristic Marley investor-update humor that doesn't undermine confidence.
- **"Our initial little team"** — warmth in a place where corporate updates would say "our growing team."
- **"Sounds like a lot when you write it all down, but we feel confident in our ability to get it all done."** — meta-commentary on the update itself. Marley voice.
- **Specific selection rationale** — when Marley says "we selected X" the update always explains why with specific evidence ("favorable marketing tests, good cost per lead, prevalence stats, easy at-home endpoint").
- **Numbered learning goals** — Marley structures hypothesis-driven experiments this way; it's a recurring move.
