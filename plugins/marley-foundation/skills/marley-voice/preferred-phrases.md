# Preferred phrases

The affirmative version of [[banned-phrases]]. These are the Marley-coded vocabulary moves that should appear when the meaning fits. Don't force them — but when you can use one without strain, use it.

## Value-proposition framing

- **"the best way"** — Marley's signature value framing. ("The best way to manage high blood pressure is to work with us.")
- **"the future of healthcare"** — used when contrasting with the legacy system.
- **"actually possible (now)"** — frame: this used to be impossible, now it isn't.
- **"coordinated, convenient, continuous"** — Marley's three c's of care.

## Care-team / model

- **"24/7"** — never "round the clock" or "anytime."
- **"team of experts"**, **"specialized care team"**, **"multi-disciplinary team"** — three flavors of the same idea. Pick by rhythm.
- **"licensed primary care physicians"** — when precision matters.
- **"on-call via message, phone or video"** — recurring care-team-access phrasing.
- **"hypertension specialists"** / **"care team of experts"** — the team is the differentiator.

## Patient-experience phrasing

- **"meds and equipment delivered to your door"** — never "shipped" or "fulfilled."
- **"home delivery"** — Marley-flavored. Not "mail order."
- **"insurance and Medicare handled for you"** — recurring phrase.
- **"clear copays"** / **"transparent copays"** — Marley's frame on the financial layer.
- **"lower stress and lower costs"** — paired benefit framing.
- **"answer, monitor and motivate"** — care-team action triad.

## Empathy register

- **"we'll be right there with you"** — Marley's late-night-worry empathy phrase.
- **"feel heard"** — what patients should feel.
- **"a healthier life"** — outcome framing (not just "better outcomes").
- **"your goal is our goal"** — partnership framing.

## Operational / investor register

- **"tl;dr"** — investor-update opening section header (lowercase).
- **"hunker down"** — operational candor for "we're going to focus and grind."
- **"come out strong"** — pair with hunker down.
- **"lots of duct tape"** — honest about v1 stitched-together state.
- **"a worthy and fun problem to solve"** — Chris's framing for hard challenges.
- **"meet and greet"** — Marley's term for non-clinical first call with a prospective patient.
- **"the pod"** / **"regional pod"** — geography concept (cluster of states served by one care team).

## Strategic-positioning phrasing

- **"virtual-first"** — never "virtual-only" or "telehealth" alone.
- **"specialized primary care"** — Marley's category name.
- **"B2C2B"** — Marley-coined inversion of B2B2C.
- **"broad in-network coverage"** — payer-strategy frame.
- **"whole patient"** — what Marley treats (not just one disease).
- **"escalation mode" / "maintenance mode"** — the two phases of patient management.

## Disease + science register (when precision matters)

- **"guideline-based blood pressure goals"** / **"guideline-indicated"** — internal/clinical/payer use only.
- **"uncomplicated, but uncontrolled"** — Marley's target patient frame (clinical contexts).
- **"chronic disease"** — preferred over "chronic illness" or "chronic condition" in strategy contexts. Use "condition" or "disease" interchangeably in patient-facing copy.

## When to bend or break a rule

The voice is a guide, not a constraint. If a sentence would land harder with a banned phrase, use the banned phrase. If "best-in-class" is the right phrase in a clinical-evidence section of a payer deck, use it. The principle is **plainspoken-confident**, not "follow this list."
