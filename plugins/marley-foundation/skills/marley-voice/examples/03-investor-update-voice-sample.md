# Investor-update voice sample

The opening sections of the May 2022 investor update, included here as a tone reference for the **investor-update register** of the Marley voice. Numerical specifics have been preserved as written; investor names and headcount details that appeared elsewhere in the update have been excluded per the redaction policy.

This is what Marley sounds like when Chris is writing to investors: same voice DNA as the consumer copy, dialed for an exec audience. Plain, direct, honest about hard things, occasionally colloquial. Not corporate.

---

## Sample: opening of "Marley Medical Investor Update — May 2022"

> **tl;dr**
>
> On April 21, we launched our first fully functional virtual primary care clinic in Wisconsin. We have now built the capabilities needed to offer virtual care products and services. We will add Illinois and Michigan shortly, and then Minnesota, Nebraska and Missouri by August 2022. Our goal is to create a Midwest regional pod, with sufficient reach, that can be served by the same clinical team, same clinical protocols and the same marketing campaigns. We will use this first region to iterate and learn, and improve our patient acquisition, our clinical offering and our patient retention.
>
> Overall, we are very happy with how the team is coming together and how it is performing. We have a great initial team that is almost complete, and we think this team can carry us for a while with no additional hires.
>
> We have not yet figured out patient acquisition, but are steadily learning, and we now have a more complete product to try to sell. Patient acquisition for these populations is a hard problem that will take a lot of tenacity, a good bit of creativity and some luck, but it is a worthy and fun problem to solve. It feels like a pretty good time to just hunker down and figure some things out, then re-emerge with all these great learnings when it's a little warmer out. We are starting to think a lot about cash positions and funding scenarios, and how to best survive winter and come out strong.

---

## What to learn from this

- **"tl;dr" lowercase** as the opening section header — Marley-flavored, not "Executive Summary."
- **Lead with what happened**, with a date and concrete artifact ("On April 21, we launched our first fully functional virtual primary care clinic in Wisconsin.")
- **Frame next steps**, not just past steps — the plan for the pod, the states.
- **Honest about the hard thing**, immediately. "We have not yet figured out patient acquisition." No hedging, no softening. Then the next sentence reframes it as worth doing.
- **Colloquial register carries warmth.** "Hunker down and figure some things out", "re-emerge…when it's a little warmer out", "come out strong" — these would be edited out of a corporate update. They are the voice.
- **No corporate hedging.** Not "we strive to" — "we will." Not "we are evaluating funding strategies" — "we are starting to think a lot about cash positions and funding scenarios."
