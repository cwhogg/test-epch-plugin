# Traction conventions — fundraise materials

How Marley frames traction in fundraise pitch decks and the Series A Memo. Drawn from the March 2024 Sapphire deck and the Series A Memo (v5, Jan 2024).

The Marley fundraise-traction frame is **specific numbers + concrete progression + honest risk paydown**.

---

## What Marley leads with

The traction story is structured around **three pillars**, in this order:

### Pillar 1: Patient growth trajectory
- Bar chart: patients enrolled by year-end
- Specific numbers with the multiplier in plain English
- *"After 10xing patient growth in 2023, we will target 2-3x growth in 2024–5."*
- Pattern: 98 (end 2022) → 1,038 (end 2023, 10x) → 3,200 target (end 2024, 3x) → 7,500 target (end 2025, 2.3x)

### Pillar 2: Clinical evidence
- **70% at guideline-based clinical goal at 6 months**
- **14.8 mmHg average BP reduction at 12 weeks** (Stage 2 HTN entry)
- Engagement metrics paired with clinical: 78% / 75% / NPS 86 / device readings / message volume
- Always cite Lancet meta-analysis for downstream-risk-reduction framing

### Pillar 3: Payer contracting evidence
- **13 payer contracts** in the Midwest
- **77% payer coverage** in active markets
- **31M covered lives** under contract
- **$75 PPPM FFS rate, 35% gross margin** as foundation
- Path to **$150 PPPM, 65% gross margin** via value-based contracts

---

## What Marley does NOT lead with in fundraise materials

- ARR alone — Marley is a primary-care medical practice, not a SaaS company. ARR is a derivative of patient count × PPPM, not the headline.
- "Million-dollar conversations" or "pipeline value" without contract specifics
- Founder narratives without quantitative anchoring
- TAM-only stories (Marley shows traction, not just opportunity)
- Vanity metrics (app downloads, marketing impressions, etc.) — these never appear in source

## Risks-paid-down vs. risks-being-underwritten framing

Marley's signature ask-slide move is this two-part frame:

**Initial funding — risks we paid down:**
- Concrete, accomplished items
- Built clinic / care model / tech platform
- Enrolled X patients
- Generated positive engagement, retention, clinical data
- Contracted with N payers / X% coverage

**New funding — risks you are underwriting:**
- The forward-looking risks the new investor is taking on
- Higher effective per-patient rates via VBC contracts
- Lower CAC via payer/provider partnership channels
- Strong unit economics in the Midwest clinic

This frame is honest and disarming. It signals:
1. We know what we've actually de-risked vs. what's still in front of us.
2. We respect that you're underwriting specific, named risks — not vague "execution."
3. We trust you to make the call on whether those risks are worth your money.

## How Marley discusses CAC + LTV

From the Sapphire deck "The Plan" slide:
- **Blended CAC < $400** as a 2024-2025 target
- LTV / CAC named as a tracked unit-economic
- CAC + payback explicitly listed alongside revenue/patient, gross margin, clinic efficiency

Earlier-period CAC is also disclosed when relevant — November 2022 update reported Facebook CAC of $1,400 (down from $2,400). The pattern is **show the trend, not just the spot number.**

## How Marley discusses the team in fundraise materials

The standard team slide pattern:

> *Our co-founders and almost ⅓ of our team came from Propeller Health, an early and pioneering digital health company, focused on respiratory. We did over 30 partnerships with payers and providers, and sold to ResMed for $250M in 2019.*

The Propeller heritage carries weight because:
1. It's a successful exit ($250M to ResMed)
2. It's payer-facing experience (>30 partnerships)
3. It's clinical / digital-health domain (not generalist startup founders)
4. ~⅓ of the Marley team came from Propeller — a *team* track record, not just a founder track record

In materials, lead with the team's relevant proof points, not generic credentials.

---

## How Marley discusses the round

The ask slide pattern:

> **"Marley Medical: $6–8M Raise, led by a16z"** (or named lead investor)

- Round size as a range, not a point estimate
- Named lead investor when one is committed
- The ask isn't elaborated with terms — those happen offline
- The round size is paired with the **risks-being-underwritten** frame on the same slide
