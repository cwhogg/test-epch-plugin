# Fundraise pitch deck — canonical narrative arc

Extracted from `fundraising decks/`, primarily the **Sapphire Ventures deck (March 14, 2024)** — the most fully realized fundraise deck in source. Cross-confirmed against **M+P Combined Deck for Pan (March 25, 2024)** and the **New Series A Deck (October 30, 2023)**.

The fundraise deck shares the **first half** of the payer deck (Marley Medical Overview) but pivots in the **second half** to investor-specific content: GTM strategy, growth trajectory, unit economics, B2B pipeline, the ask. The transition is:
- **Same:** what Marley is, the problem, the model, clinical evidence
- **Different:** B2C2B GTM strategy, contract upgrade path, the platform roadmap (AI/automation), the ask

## Canonical structure

### Slide 1 — Cover
- **Marley Medical** wordmark
- **Prepared for [Firm Name]**
- **Date**

### Slide 2 — Welcome / What Marley is
Same three-bullet definition as the payer deck:
- We are a virtual-first medical practice designed specifically for patients with cardiometabolic disease
- We manage hypertension, type 2 diabetes, lipid disorders and obesity/overweight with a team-based and integrated care model
- We currently bill fee-for-service, but are increasingly contracting for outcomes-based contracts

### Slide 3 — Section opener: "Virtual-first care is transforming how we manage patients with chronic disease"
Large-type section opener. Frames the macro thesis the investor is being asked to underwrite.

### Slide 4 — The problem (hero stats grid)
Same hero stats as payer deck:
- 60+% of patients with cardiometabolic disease are uncontrolled
- 2/3 of hospitalizations are avoidable
- <7% optimal cardiometabolic health
- 26+ days to see a provider
- 23% of patients = 71% of healthcare spend

### Slide 5 — PCPs are overburdened
26.7 hours/day stat. Same as payer deck.

### Slide 6 — We are creating a new category of healthcare delivery
Same positioning visual as payer deck: complex cardiometabolic patients → new category → comprehensive & integrated care for chronic disease (paid for outcomes).

### Slide 7 — **B2C2B GTM strategy (5 steps)** [fundraise-specific]
Visual 5-step diagram:
1. **Acquire complex and expensive patients** — 1,250 patients enrolled to date; 3,200 by EOY
2. **Build clinic, care model, and tech platform** — including "Fetch", Marley's data-driven task list + patient engagement platform for clinicians
3. **Generate evidence to validate our care model** — 70% of patients at goal at 6 months
4. **Payer network contracting + billing fee-for-service** — Contracted with 13 payers; 77% payer coverage
5. **Pitch payers and risk-taking providers for value-based contracts** — Capitation, P4P, Shared Savings. Signed first partnership with Cap Rate + Shared Savings.

### Slide 8 — Patient growth trajectory
- 2x bar chart showing patient count growth
- 98 (end 2022) → 1,038 (end 2023, 10x) → 3,200 (end 2024, 3x target) → 7,500 (end 2025, 2.3x target)
- Plus current panel profile (Age, Chronic conditions, Mental health %, Obesity %)

### Slide 9 — The integrated cardiometabolic clinic we built
Three-bullet definition (same as payer deck slide 7).

### Slide 10 — Three ways patients come to Marley
Same as payer deck. Self-refer / PCP refer / Payer identify.

### Slide 11 — Patient journey
Same as payer deck. Lead → Video → Ship Kit → Continuous Monitoring → Proactive outreach + async messaging → Follow-up.

### Slide 12 — Engagement metrics
Same as payer deck: 78% active / 75% retention / NPS 86 / device readings / messages / access time.

### Slide 13 — **The future of our platform (AI / automation roadmap)** [fundraise-specific]
"Our data-driven, asynchronous care model lends itself to tech-enablement and automation. And AI."

**Automated Reminders (Fetch):** Device-data check → automated reminders → support task → postponed clinical review → 8 hours/week of clinic time saved (estimated).

**LLM-Assisted Functions:**
- Inbound message categorization
- Generation of draft response messages
- Generative agent for medication titration
- Generative agent for health coaching

### Slide 14 — Data-driven care model + Propeller heritage
Same as payer deck — including Propeller's 57% / 33% ED+Hosp reductions in asthma/COPD as comparable evidence.

### Slide 15 — Recent patient case example
Hypertensive Crisis (182/117) → Controlled BP (126/72) in 23 days. Same case as payer deck.

### Slide 16 — Clinical results
70% at goal at 6mo / 14.8 mmHg avg reduction / Lancet downstream-risk-reduction stats. Same as payer deck.

### Slide 17 — **Payer contracts: 13 payers, 77% coverage, 31M covered lives** [fundraise-specific]
- 13 top Midwest payers contracted
- 77% payer coverage in active markets
- 31M covered lives
- **$75 PPPM FFS rate** at **35% gross margin** — "the foundation of our business model"

### Slide 18 — **Contract upgrade path: FFS → outcomes-based** [fundraise-specific]
Visual diagram showing margin/rate progression:
- $75 PPPM @ 35% gross margin (FFS foundation)
- $100 PPPM @ 50% gross margin (Direct Contracting)
- $125 PPPM @ 59% gross margin
- $150 PPPM @ 65% gross margin (Capitation)

"Each contract will increase margins and provide a new patient enrollment channel."

### Slide 19 — Marley meets payer needs
Same as payer deck slide 16 — engagement, star ratings, MLR framing.

### Slide 20 — **B2B targets pipeline** [fundraise-specific]
"We know our B2B targets, and we have good FFS → VBC momentum."
Named pipeline targets: **Humana, Aledade, Oak Street, Agilon, Stanford, UCSF, Lilly, Novo Nordisk.**

### Slide 21 — **The Plan** (growth / revenue / partnerships / unit economics targets) [fundraise-specific]
Four-quadrant target slide:
- **Growth:** 3,200 patients by end of 2024 (3x); 7,500 by end of 2025 (2.4x); Blended CAC < $400
- **Revenue:** $2.2M ARR by end of 2024; $7.1M ARR by end of 2025; Average $100 PPPM by end of 2025
- **Partnerships:** Payers / Health Systems / Other providers and clinical partners
- **Unit economics:** Revenue/patient, Clinic efficiency, Gross margin, CAC + payback, LTV/CAC

### Slide 22 — Team + Propeller heritage / fundraising track record
"Our co-founders and almost ⅓ of our team came from Propeller Health, an early and pioneering digital health company, focused on respiratory. We did over 30 partnerships with payers and providers, and sold to ResMed for $250M in 2019."

Logos: Marley investors so far + Propeller co-founders' track record.

### Slide 23 — **The Ask** [fundraise-specific]
- "**Marley Medical: $6–8M Raise, led by a16z**"
- **Initial funding — risks we paid down:**
  - We built our clinic, clinical model and technology platform
  - We enrolled 1,250+ patients
  - We generated positive engagement, retention and clinical data
  - We contracted with 13 different payers for 77% payer coverage
- **New funding — risks you are underwriting:**
  - We can contract with payers and risk-taking providers for higher effective per-patient rates
  - We can partner with payers and providers for lower CAC patient leads
  - We can demonstrate strong unit economics in our Midwest clinic

## Investor-specific customization

Each fundraise deck is **prepared for a specific named firm**. Customize:
- Cover slide (firm name + date)
- "The Ask" slide: replace named lead investor with the actual lead, adjust raise size if different conversation
- Slide 22 — investor logos / track-record framing tuned to whether the firm cares more about consumer (Roman/Hims framing), clinical (Galileo/Firefly), or value-capture (Oak/ChenMed)
- B2B pipeline (Slide 20) — emphasize targets relevant to the firm's portfolio or thesis

## Don't collide with "investor update" memo
A fundraise pitch deck is a **visual deck for a specific named round** (Series A, Series B, etc.) addressed to a specific firm or named round-lead.
An "investor update" is a **written periodic memo** sent to existing investors on a recurring cadence.

The two are entirely different artifacts. See `../memo/audience-cues.md` for the distinction.
