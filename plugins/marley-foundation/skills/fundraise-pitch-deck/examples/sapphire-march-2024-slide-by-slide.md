## What to learn from this example

- **The first half mirrors the payer deck** — what Marley is, problem, model, evidence — but is faster (the investor wants to get to the GTM and traction).
- **B2C2B GTM (Slide 7)** is the signature investor-facing strategy slide — 5 steps with concrete metrics attached to each.
- **Growth chart (Slide 8) is the headline traction slide** — bar chart of patients by year with multipliers in plain English.
- **The AI/platform slide (Slide 13)** is more about *thesis credibility* (we have a coherent automation roadmap) than about *what AI we've built today.* It's honest — Fetch is built; LLM-assisted functions are roadmap.
- **Contract upgrade path (Slide 18)** is Marley's signature business-model visualization — turns "FFS → VBC" from buzzwords into a margin progression.
- **The Plan (Slide 21)** is a forward-looking commitment slide — quarter-by-quarter outcomes investors can hold Marley to.
- **The Ask (Slide 23) is the most distinctive Marley fundraise move:** risks-paid-down vs. risks-being-underwritten. Disarming, honest, respect-the-investor framing.
