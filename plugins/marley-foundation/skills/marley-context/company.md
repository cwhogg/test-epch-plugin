# Marley Medical — Company Overview

## What Marley is

Marley Medical is a **virtual-first medical practice designed specifically for patients with cardiometabolic disease.** Marley manages **hypertension, type 2 diabetes, lipid disorders, and obesity/overweight** with a team-based, integrated care model. The company started with hypertension as its lead indication (2021–2022) and expanded into the broader cardiometabolic scope as the model matured (visible in 2023–2024 payer and fundraise decks).

Marley is built around the thesis that we can manage chronic disease dramatically better than the current system if we redesign the care experience from scratch around the patient instead of around the incumbent provider, payer, pharmacy, and hospital.

As of the March 2024 fundraise deck: **1,250 patients enrolled (target 3,200 by EOY 2024 / 7,500 by EOY 2025)**, operating in 6 states with clinicians licensed in 26 more, contracted with 13 payers for 77% coverage and 31M covered lives, $75 PPPM FFS rate at 35% gross margin, currently raising $6–8M led by a16z.

## Mission

Build the leading and most trusted brand for the treatment of common chronic diseases — starting with high blood pressure, then high cholesterol and orally-treated type 2 diabetes, then expanding into other diseases that fit the model.

## What Marley actually does for a patient

1. Patient signs up online via direct marketing (Facebook is the largest channel as of late 2022).
2. Patient meets their care team — a licensed primary care physician plus a multi-disciplinary team (clinical pharmacist, RN, care coordinator).
3. Marley ships a cellular Bluetooth blood-pressure cuff to the patient's home.
4. Patient takes BP readings at home; readings flow to the care team automatically.
5. Care team interacts with patient mostly **asynchronously via messaging**, with synchronous phone/video visits when needed.
6. Care team manages medications — adjusting, optimizing, prescribing — with medicines delivered to the patient's door.
7. Care team iterates rapidly on the treatment plan until the patient reaches BP goal (escalation mode), then maintains them at goal (maintenance mode).
8. Marley handles insurance and Medicare billing; patient pays a transparent co-pay structure.

## What makes Marley different — the five-point positioning

These five points are the canonical "How is Marley different" statement from the Series Seed narrative:

1. **Specialized primary care.** Primary care focused on specific patient segments only, not general population.
2. **B2C2B.** Direct patient acquisition first, then favorable B2B payer contracts — not the standard B2B2C health-tech model.
3. **Virtual-first.** Marley does not build physical offices. We partner for in-person care when needed.
4. **Broad in-network coverage.** Paid as a traditional in-network provider, not via narrow B2B carve-outs.
5. **Multi-plan.** Planning for a mix of payers and payment models, including risk models, like a normal PCP practice.

## Comparable companies and how Marley positions against them

From the Series Seed narrative:
- **Brand & patient acquisition** — like Roman / Hims (direct-to-consumer health brands)
- **Clinical care model** — like Galileo / Firefly (virtual care teams)
- **Value capture** — like Oak Street Health / ChenMed (primary-care risk-based payment)

Marley is none of these alone; the bet is that combining all three is the right configuration for chronic-disease primary care.

## Who founded Marley

Chris Hogg (CEO). The founding concept doc dates to June 2020. Marley was named in or before May 2021. Prior to Marley, Chris worked at **Propeller Health** (where he saw the limitations of the B2B2C model — explicitly the motivation for the B2C2B inversion) and **Gilead** (where he learned the discipline of rigorous decision memos).

The team has grown to include co-founders / executives David Hubanks (COO), Joe Slavinsky (CBO), and a clinical team led by Dr. Rachel Bennett with PharmD Rebecca Liggon, RN Chelsea Biel, and others.
